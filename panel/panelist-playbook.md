This playbook expands on the Panelist Code of Conduct in the SEASON 1 DISCUSSION PANEL GUIDE. It provides clear guidance on **what to say** (best practices with examples) and **what not to say** (common pitfalls to avoid) during threads, Spaces, and interactions. The goal is to foster high-quality, signal-aligned discussions that model authentic growth, while tying into timed signals (immediate, short-term, long-term, network authority) and flexible formations (e.g., 5:3:2:1+).  

As a panelist, your contributions should demonstrate expertise, encourage community participation, and align with X's recommendation systems—boosting For You visibility through depth, citations, and loops. Remember: You're not just sharing; you're building surplus value for all.

## 1. Preparation Before Sessions/Spaces
Prepare to lead with authority. Review the session's tactical system (e.g., Build-Up Tactics) and timed signals.

### What to Say/Do
- **Research and Prep Evidence:** Gather real X examples, screenshots, or data tied to signals.  
  *Example Prep Note:* "For Build-Up Tactics, I'll share how immediate profile clicks (0-10 min) led to a 3-turn conversation engagement (short-term), boosting my follow retention by 25% (long-term)."
- **Align with Formation:** Choose a personal formation variant and explain its signal ties.  
  *Example:* "I'm using a 4:3:3:1+ for this session—emphasizing midfield quotes for trust-building."
- **Engage Early:** Respond to host tags within 2-4 hours to model immediate signals.

### What Not to Say/Do
- **Avoid Generic Overviews:** Don't recycle unoriginal content without personalization.  
  *Bad Example:* "Build-up is important." (Lacks depth; no signals.)
- **Skip Self-Promotion Prep:** Don't plan pitches; focus on value.  
  *Bad Prep:* "I'll mention my course on growth hacks."
- **Ignore Time Frames:** Don't overlook signals—always tie tactics to immediate/short/long-term.

## 2. During Thread Discussions
Threads are standalone for visibility. Use quote-replies to build sequential depth (short-term threading signals).

### What to Say
- **Lead with Insights Tied to Signals/Formations:** Start responses with evidence-based analysis.  
  *Good Example (Session 1: Build-Up):* "In Low Block Build-Up, I prioritize immediate dwell instances (0-10 min) on profiles before replying. This avoided bot suspicion and led to 15% higher bookmark rates (short-term) in my AI niche, forming content clusters over 30 days."
- **Build on Others:** Credit and extend ideas to create mutual loops.  
  *Good Example:* "@Panelist2's point on Tiki-Taka is spot-on—adding my twist: Use staggered replies (5-15 min gaps) to mimic natural play, enhancing conversation engagement without patterns."
- **Prompt Community:** Ask open questions to drive replies/quotes.  
  *Good Example:* "How has Double Pivot Control helped your long-term follow retention? Share a screenshot if possible!"
- **Disagree Constructively:** Challenge with data, not opinion.  
  *Good Example:* "While Midfield Overload works for collaborative niches, in high-risk politics, I'd adapt to 5:4:1:1+ for stronger guards—my data shows 20% lower spam flags."

### What Not to Say
- **Generic Agreements/Disagreements:** Avoid low-depth filler.  
  *Bad Example:* "Great idea!" or "I disagree." (No value; ignores signals like comment depth.)
- **Off-Topic Tangents:** Stick to the session's tactics.  
  *Bad Example:* "Speaking of growth, check my LinkedIn for more tips." (Promotional; derails.)
- **Unsubstantiated Claims:** No vague boasts without evidence.  
  *Bad Example:* "This tactic always works." (Lacks citations; weak network authority.)
- **Spam-Like Patterns:** Don't post bursts or copy-paste.  
  *Bad Example:* Repeating the same reply across threads (triggers guards).

## 3. During X Spaces (Real-Time Engagement)
Spaces amplify voice authenticity (harder to spam) and create FOMO. Focus on 60-min structure: Intros, deep-dives, Q&A.

### What to Say
- **Introduce with Expertise:** Tie your background to signals/formations.  
  *Good Example (Space 1 Intro):* "As a GEOCoLab strategist, I've tested 4:3:3:1+ in tech niches—focusing on short-term quote quality to build long-term citation frequency."
- **Share Real-Time Examples:** Use voice for storytelling, backed by signals.  
  *Good Example:* "In a recent campaign, immediate like speed (capped at 3/min) transitioned to bookmark actions, resulting in 40% better retention loops."
- **Facilitate Q&A:** Rotate to community, crediting inputs.  
  *Good Example:* "Great question from @UserX on OON expansion—building on that, Wing Play leverages network authority via cross-platform citations."
- **Wrap with Takeaways:** Summarize for clips/quote-tweets.  
  *Good Example:* "Key from today: Adapt formations to timed signals for sustainable wins."

### What Not to Say
- **Monologue Without Interaction:** Don't dominate; let others speak.  
  *Bad Example:* Rambling for 5+ min without pausing (breaks loops; low engagement).
- **Off-Script Promotion:** No plugs.  
  *Bad Example:* "For more, buy my ebook." (Violates non-promotional rule.)
- **Unprepared Filler:** Avoid umms or vague responses.  
  *Bad Example:* "Uh, yeah, signals are important somehow." (Lacks depth; poor authenticity.)
- **Ignore Time Cues:** Don't overrun segments.  
  *Bad Example:* Continuing after host signals wrap-up (disrupts flow).

## 4. Post-Session/Overall Etiquette
Contribute to synthesis and long-term impact.

### What to Say/Do
- **Follow-Up in Threads:** Quote your Space clips or add reflections.  
  *Good Example:* "Post-Space insight: False 9 variants boost verified follower ratios (3x weight) for authority—here's my data."
- **Commit Publicly:** Share one adopted tactic.  
  *Good Example:* "My pledge: Implement League Consistency with daily short-term bookmarks for better clusters."
- **Network Ethically:** DM thanks or collab ideas post-series.  
  *Good Example:* "Loved your No. 9 Focus take—let's explore a joint thread on citations?"
- **Provide Feedback:** To host privately for improvements.

### What Not to Say/Do
- **Ghost After Sessions:** Don't disappear—maintain loops.  
  *Bad Example:* No response to synthesis tags (misses long-term signals).
- **Negative Public Critique:** Handle issues privately.  
  *Bad Example:* Tweeting "Session was off-track." (Harms discourse.)
- **Over-Share Personal Metrics:** Avoid bragging without context.  
  *Bad Example:* "I gained 10K followers!" (Focus on tactics, not vanity.)
- **Violate Inclusivity:** No exclusionary language.  
  *Bad Example:* "Only big accounts get this." (Discourages diverse voices.)

## Quick Reference: Do's and Don'ts Checklist
### Do's (What to Say)
- Tie everything to formations/signals (e.g., "This tactic enhances short-term threading for long-term loops").
- Use evidence (screenshots/data/citations).
- Credit/build on others (@tags/quotes).
- Ask engaging questions.
- Model the 5:3:2:1+ in your posts.

### Don'ts (What Not to Say)
- Generic/vague (add depth/min 2 sentences).
- Promotional/self-focused.
- Off-topic or derailing.
- Unsubstantiated/opinion-only.
- Spammy/repetitive.
